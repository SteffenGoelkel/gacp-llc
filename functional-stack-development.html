Functional Stack Development

Most botanical brands fail not at marketing but at architecture. A formula either over-relies on a single hero ingredient, or stacks unrelated extracts hoping for a synergy the dosing never delivers. We build the architecture before we sell the inputs — multi-layer ingredient stacks designed around a defined functional outcome, with regulatory framing, supplier verification, and dosing logic locked in before a single batch is run.

i. Layer One

Foundation

Structural and Qi-tonic base. The systemic layer that defines what the formula does at a metabolic level — primary tonics, baseline adaptogenic support, the energetic substrate everything else acts upon.

ii. Layer Two

Restoration

Adaptogenic resilience. The buffering layer that protects the HPA axis, modulates stress response, and prevents the active layer from depleting reserves over a daily-use horizon.

iii. Layer Three

Activation

Functional drive — the targeted-effect layer. Yang tonics, nootropic actives, mood modulators, or whatever pathway the brief calls for, standardized to a defined bioactive content with a stated dose rationale.

iv. Layer Four

Delivery

Bioavailability and circulation — the layer most stacks forget. Piperine, lecithin matrices, water-soluble complexes, or excipient strategy that ensures the actives actually reach target tissue at the dose claimed on the label.

How an engagement moves

Five Stages · 6–10 Weeks
01

Brief intake

What is the functional positioning? What is the price ceiling? What is the regulatory envelope — DSHEA structure/function only, GRAS-required, novel-food-cleared? We don't start with ingredients. We start with constraints. A clean brief halves the development timeline.

02

Architecture proposal

A layered formula skeleton — foundation, restoration, activation, delivery. Each slot specified by function before any specific ingredient is named. This is where most consultancies start and finish; for us it is the second of five steps.

03

Sourcing & specification

A supplier shortlist for each slot. CoA review, standardization grade, supply continuity, price stability. Where we hold direct supplier relationships — sceletium chemovars from South Africa, the Vita Forte freeze-dried superfood line, GACP-cultivated kanna — the chain is already verified and the lead times are known.

04

Compliance & claim mapping

Every ingredient mapped to a compliant claim. DSHEA structure/function language, cross-checked against the 1998 CRN list and AHPA Herbs of Commerce. Contraindication review for blood-thinning, MAOI, and hypotensive interactions, plus a stimulant-load audit. We flag what your label cannot say before you commission packaging.

05

Production-ready specification

Full bill of materials, dose per serving, manufacturing notes, supplier contacts, and a sample run for sensory and stability validation. You leave with a document a contract manufacturer can quote against — not a concept deck.

Optional extension — if you'd rather not run that pathway yourself, GACP can. We operate the full sourcing and production handoff under a single agreement: ingredient procurement, contract-manufacturer coordination, CoA management, and finished-goods logistics. Some clients use the spec as a procurement tool; others hand us the whole pathway.
